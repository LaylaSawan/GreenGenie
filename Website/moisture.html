Moisture

What is Moisture

Soil moisture sensors help us quantify the amount of water in the compost. This is accomplished by inserting two electrodes into soil. The moisture in the compost is measured indirectly by measuring the conductivity of the soil. If the soil is more conductive it means that it is more moist, resulting in a higher reading.

Why is Moisture Important in Composting

Moisture is an important factor in composting because it helps to break down the organic matter and create a suitable environment for microorganisms to thrive. If the compost pile is too dry, then the microorganisms that break down the organic matter will not be able to survive, and the composting process will slow down or stop altogether.

image

Ideal Values

The ideal moisture content for composting is around 40-60%. This level of moisture helps to create a healthy environment for microorganisms to break down the organic matter into nutrient-rich soil. Additionally, moisture helps to regulate the temperature in the compost pile.
